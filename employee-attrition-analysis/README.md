# Employee Attrition Analysis

This project analyzes employee attrition using the **IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition & Performance** dataset.

The goal is to identify which factors are most strongly associated with employees leaving the company and to suggest practical HR actions to improve retention.

## Project Files

- **Employee_Attrition_Report.pdf** — final analytical report with charts, findings, and HR recommendations.
- **employee_attrition_analysis.ipynb** — Python notebook with data cleaning, exploratory analysis, visualizations, and statistical tests.
- **WA_Fn-UseC_-HR-Employee-Attrition.csv** — source dataset.

## Tools Used

- Python
- pandas
- numpy
- matplotlib
- seaborn
- scipy
- SQL / BigQuery
- Jupyter Notebook

## Key Findings

- Overtime is the strongest attrition risk factor.
- Employees in their first year are much more likely to leave.
- Lower income, junior roles, and lack of stock options increase attrition risk.
- Low job satisfaction and poor work-life balance compound the risk.
- Gender is not a statistically significant attrition driver.

## Main Business Insight

Attrition is not random.

The highest-risk employees are those who combine several risk factors, especially:

- overtime
- low income
- early tenure
- no stock options

## HR Recommendations

- Reduce and compensate overtime.
- Improve onboarding during the first 12 months.
- Review pay for high-risk roles.
- Extend retention incentives to junior and front-line employees.
- Monitor satisfaction and work-life balance through regular pulse surveys.
